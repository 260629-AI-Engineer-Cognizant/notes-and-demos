# Revature Pro Portfolio Update Guide

## Purpose

This guide is meant to help you update your Revature Pro portfolio so that it is clear, professional, client-ready, and aligned with the expectations for this training cohort.

Your portfolio should highlight your current technical skills, your Revature training projects, and your ability to explain your work clearly. The portfolio should be written in a way that makes it easy for a client, recruiter, or reviewer to understand what you have learned, what you have built, and what role you played in each project.

---

# Creating Your Portfolio in Revature Pro

Create your portfolio in **Revature Pro** from the drop-down menu under your name.

## Portfolio Naming Convention

Use the following naming format:

```text
First Name - Last Name - Version#
```

Example:

```text
Jane - Smith - v1
```

## Required Portfolio Settings

Make sure the following settings are enabled:

- Your portfolio is checked as **Primary**
- **Show Globally** is checked for any section that should appear in the published version
- Sections that should not be publicly visible are hidden appropriately

# About Me Section

The **About Me** section is one of the most important parts of the portfolio because it showcases who you are as a professional.

This section should:

- Be clear, concise, and professional
- Use descriptive language and action verbs
- Explain your technical focus
- Highlight your strengths and interests
- Help the reader understand what makes you a strong candidate

## Requirements

Your About Me section should include:

- **3-5 full sentences**
- **3-5 interesting facts or professional highlights**
- A strong emphasis on your technical growth, project experience, and career direction

## What to Include

You may include details such as:

- Your interest in AI, machine learning, software engineering, or data-driven applications
- Your experience working with Python, TensorFlow, SQL, cloud tools, or AI frameworks
- Your ability to learn quickly and build projects from requirements
- Your collaboration, communication, or problem-solving skills
- Any relevant background that supports your target role

# Contact Information

For privacy and professional presentation, make sure the following fields are **not shown on the portfolio**:

- Email
- Contact number / phone number

# Education and Certifications

Only include degrees and certifications that have actually been awarded.

## Requirements

- Do not list degrees or certifications that are incomplete or in progress unless specifically instructed otherwise
- Include all awarded degrees
- If you have a master's degree, include your bachelor's degree as well
- Include awarded certifications only

## Examples

Appropriate entries:

- Bachelor of Science in Computer Science
- Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics
- Master of Science in Data Analytics
- AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner
- Oracle Certified Associate

Do not list certifications or degrees that you have only started, are currently studying for, or plan to complete in the future.

## Skill Rating and Time Guidance

Associates may adjust the amount of time listed based on their own experience.

A suggested approach is to rate each technology from **1-4**, then multiply that rating by **3** to estimate months of experience.

Do not list more than **12 months** for any technology unless you have actual experience beyond that.

| Rating | Estimated Months |
|---:|---:|
| 1 | 3 months |
| 2 | 6 months |
| 3 | 9 months |
| 4 | 12 months |

## Example

| Skill | Rating | Months |
|---|---:|---:|
| Python | 4 | 12 months |
| SQL | 3 | 9 months |
| TensorFlow | 2 | 6 months |
| LangChain | 1 | 3 months |

# Work Experience

The Work Experience section should be handled carefully.

## What to Include

Include:

- Previous client placements, if applicable

## What Not to Include

Do not include unrelated previous work experience in the main Work Experience section.

## Important Notes

- Previous client placements should be included
- Previous non-technical work history should generally not be included in the main Work Experience section
- If previous work history is related to the role, it may be included under **Other Experiences**

---

# Other Experiences

Use the **Other Experiences** section for previous work history only if it is relevant.

Relevant experience may include:

- Technical roles
- Data-related roles
- Software development roles
- IT support roles
- Teaching, training, mentoring, or leadership experience
- Roles involving analysis, automation, reporting, or process improvement

Avoid adding unrelated roles unless they help tell a stronger professional story.

## Project Requirements

For each project, include:

- 5-8 bullet points describing your role and responsibilities

# Top 5 Skills Used in Each Project

Each project should include the top 5 technologies or skills used.

These should match the actual work performed in the project.


# Project Roles and Responsibilities

Associates are responsible for writing their own project roles and responsibilities.

Each project should include **5 bullet points** explaining what you contributed.

These bullet points should be descriptive and should use action verbs.

## Strong Action Verbs

Use verbs such as:

- Built
- Designed
- Developed
- Implemented
- Preprocessed
- Trained
- Evaluated
- Tuned
- Tested
- Analyzed
- Integrated
- Documented
- Collaborated
- Presented
- Refactored

---

## Current Project Responsibility Examples

For the current machine learning / TensorFlow project, your responsibilities may include items similar to the following:

- Preprocessed and transformed input data to prepare it for machine learning model training and evaluation.
- Built and trained TensorFlow-based neural network models to solve the assigned prediction or classification task.
- Tested multiple model configurations by adjusting architecture, training parameters, and evaluation metrics.
- Evaluated model performance using validation results, accuracy, loss, and other relevant metrics to identify areas for improvement.
- Tuned preprocessing steps, model structure, and training settings to improve reliability and overall performance.
